# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- **Customers**: Turkish-speaking men in Darıca seeking premium haircut & beard care. They land on the site from Instagram/Google, often on mobile, and want to book an appointment in under a minute without calling.
- **Salon owner (admin)**: Manages appointments, barbers, working hours, holidays, and revenue from a desktop dashboard during work hours.
- **Barbers**: Check their daily schedule and mark appointment outcomes (arrived / no-show) on a phone or tablet between clients.

## Product Purpose

Online booking platform for Abdurrahman Çelik Exclusive Salon, a premium barber shop in Darıca, Turkey. The public site is the salon's digital storefront: it must convey luxury and trust strongly enough that a first-time visitor books without hesitation. The dashboards keep daily operations (scheduling, no-shows, revenue) running without phone calls or paper.

Success: appointments booked online instead of by phone, zero double-bookings, and a brand impression that justifies premium pricing.

## Brand Personality

Exclusive, masterful, warm. The tone of a tailor's atelier, not a tech startup: confident serif display type (Cormorant Garamond), restrained layouts, a single deep red as the signature. Turkish language throughout (tr_TR).

## Anti-references

- **Trendy hipster barber clichés**: no mustache icons, vintage badges, distressed textures, chalkboard aesthetics.
- **Cheap local-business template sites**: no stock-photo heroes, cluttered widgets, or low-trust patterns that undermine premium pricing.
- Generic SaaS gloss is acceptable in dashboards but must never leak into customer-facing pages.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the storefront**: every customer-facing screen must look like it belongs to a premium salon; brand register leads, dashboards follow with shared tokens.
2. **Booking friction is brand damage**: readability, tap targets, and step clarity in the booking flow outrank decorative flourishes.
3. **One red, used sparingly**: the signature red carries identity in small, deliberate doses (CTAs, active states), never as wallpaper.
4. **Editorial restraint over ornament**: hierarchy through serif/sans contrast and spacing, not effects, glows, or textures.
5. **Trust through clarity**: real barber names, real reviews, real hours; the design's job is to make truth legible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1.
- Mobile-first for customers and barbers; admin optimized for desktop.
- Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations.
- Turkish locale conventions (dates, currency ₺).
